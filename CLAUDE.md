## Hackathon Challenge: Urban Administrative Efficiency (PQRS Optimization)

### Project Overview
The objective of this challenge is to revolutionize the management of **PQRS** (Petitions, Complaints, Claims, and Suggestions) within a municipal Secretariat (Alcaldía). Currently, the administration is overwhelmed by a massive volume of citizen requests, leading to response times ranging from **10 to 15 business days**. The goal is to design a logic-driven, optimized process—culminating in a Minimum Viable Product (MVP)—that reduces this response window to a maximum of **3 business days**.

### Core Objectives
 * **Operational Optimization:** Redesign the process logic to handle high-frequency data and requests without increasing administrative overhead.
 * **Drastic Latency Reduction:** Move the needle from the current 10–15 day turnaround to a 3-day high-efficiency model.
 * **Scalability:** Develop a solution that is applicable not only to the primary Secretariat but also adaptable for other municipal entities and local governments facing similar bottlenecks.
 * **Citizen-Centric Design:** Ensure the end-user (the citizen) receives timely, accurate, and transparent updates on their requests.

### Key Challenges to Address
 1. **High-Volume Triage:** How can we use logic or automation to categorize, prioritize, and route massive amounts of incoming PQRS data to the correct departments instantly?
 2. **Bottleneck Identification:** Analyze current operational delays to identify where requests typically "stall" during the 15-day cycle.
 3. **Process Automation vs. Human Oversight:** Define which parts of the response process can be automated for speed and which require human validation for legal and administrative accuracy.
 4. **Resource Allocation:** Suggest how the Secretariat can better distribute its human and technical resources to maintain the 3-day goal during peak periods.
 
### Desired Output (MVP Focus)
You should assist in conceptualizing a solution that moves beyond theoretical ideas into a functional **MVP**. This includes:
 * A clear **data flow architecture** for incoming requests.
 * A **priority logic framework** (e.g., distinguishing an urgent infrastructure "Claim" from a general "Suggestion").
 * A **tracking and alert system** to ensure no request exceeds the new 3-day threshold.


> **Context Note:** You are acting as a lead technical consultant for a government hackathon team. Your goal is to provide highly efficient, scalable, and logic-based solutions that prioritize administrative speed and citizen satisfaction. Avoid "fluff"; focus on actionable process logic and structural improvements.
